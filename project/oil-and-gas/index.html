Oil and Gas Production

Apr 27, 2016
Photo by Tianshu Chen

A lot of recent research has uncovered the evident impact of oil and gas (O&NG) production in ozone chemistry in the U.S. with lacking information for other countries. To investigate the influence of O&NG production on air quality in China, we conducted 2 intensive field campaigns during February-April 2016 and June-July 2017 in the Yellow River Delta. A large suite of chemical and meteorological parameters was in situ observed and used as constraint for a photochemistry box model to quantify the air quality effects. Furthermore, we collected source samples near oil extraction machines to depict a source profile of VOCs in China oil field. Very high VOCs level was captured in the station with evident character of the emission of the petrochemical industry and leakage of storage and transportation processes. Several photochemical episodes were observed with the continuous impact of VOCs from O&NG extraction emissions. We also conducted a 1-day simultaneous measurement in oilfield and station to directly verify such influence.
